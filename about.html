ABOUT ME

When it comes to my education, I hold a bachelor's degree in social animation, a master's degree in resocialization pedagogy with elements of psychotherapy, and a postgraduate degree in psychosomatics and somatopsychology. Additionally, I have completed a two-level training in Solution-Focused Therapy. During my studies, I was a member of the Scientific Sexology Circle for two years. My bachelor's thesis focused on stereotypes towards lesbians, while my master's thesis explored how foreigners perceive the identity of Gdynia’s residents in urban space. At one point, I even considered a doctoral path, but life outside academia captivated me, leading me to become a therapist and a companion in change rather than a researcher.

I have also participated in international training programs, mainly focused on embodiment (primarily bodywork methods, mindfulness, and nature-based personal development techniques). I have completed a massage course and attended various workshops to enhance my qualifications and personal growth (including motivational interviewing, anti-discrimination training, and an introduction to Dance Movement Therapy).

I am a circle woman, a student of Tanna Jakubowicz-Mount and Mona Cichoń. For two years, I participated in the developmental process "Guides on the Path//My Medicine," led by Tanna. During this time, I underwent my initiation process and received the name Flora Flow—"the one who touches with her heart." In this way, I became my own guide in discovering my inner medicine and do my best to support others, especially women, in uncovering their power and gifts. For the past four years, I have been leading a women's development group.

I am a member of the Educational Cha(lle)nge Association, where we strive to transform education through workshops for students and teachers, as well as annual conferences.

I collaborate with the Pomeranian Center for Psychotherapy and Psychoeducation, the Children's Development Studio “Alterway,” the Youth Cooperation Center, and the Emigration Museum. In the past, I have facilitated processes for organizations such as TEB Edukacja, Dovista, LPP, Arla, Lifeworks, and Citi.

Throughout my life, I have done many different things... I have worked as a nanny, an art teacher, a caregiver in a Daily Support Facility, and as a project coordinator and trainer at CWM. I have conducted intimacy workshops in Riga, practiced Contact Improvisation dance, and led workshops on identity. In the past, I also taught djembe drumming and led archery workshops.

I believe that these diverse experiences allow me to see the world from multiple perspectives.
